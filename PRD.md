## 1. What We Are

**Spicy Carrot is a business digitization agency for independent hospitality businesses.** We use AI agents to digitize the entire operation — marketing, operations, and finance — so the owner never has to touch a computer.

**Tagline:** "Less admin. More laughing."

**The promise:** A hospitality owner opens their phone in the morning and sees yesterday's revenue, today's staff checklist, trending reviews, and what to order. Zero computer time. We make that happen.

**Three layers of digitization:**

| Layer | Name | What It Covers |
|---|---|---|
| **Layer 1 — Be Found** | Marketing & Visibility | GEO, AI visibility, SEO, directories, social, website, review management, multilingual content |
| **Layer 2 — Run Clean** | Operations | Staff checklists, SOPs, inventory tracking, menu engineering, scheduling, time tracking, opening/closing procedures |
| **Layer 3 — See Clearly** | Finance & Intelligence | P&L visibility, cash flow, supplier cost tracking, KPI dashboards, weekly CFO brief, POS reconciliation |

**What we are:**
- AI × Hospitality. Boutique hotels, restaurants, cafés, event spaces.
- A managed service, not SaaS. Humans set direction, agents execute.
- Hospitality people who use AI — not tech people selling to hospitality.
- The full stack: we don't just make you visible, we make your business run better and make the numbers legible.

**What we are NOT:**

- Just an SEO/marketing shop with a nice logo

## 2. Target Market

### Who

Independent hospitality businesses in tourist-heavy European regions. Starting with Costa Brava / Catalunya, expanding along Mediterranean tourist corridors.

### Size

- 5–50 employees
- €200K–€5M annual revenue
- Owner-operated or small management team
- NOT micro (1–3 staff) — they can't afford us
- NOT chains — they have in-house teams

### Pain

1. **Invisible to AI.** ChatGPT, Perplexity, Gemini, Claude are becoming how travelers discover places. Most hospitality businesses don't exist in these systems.
2. **No time.** Owners work 12–16 hour days. They can't manage websites, SEO, reviews, social, directories — let alone track costs, build checklists, or reconcile their POS.
3. **Overpaying or underserving.** Traditional agencies charge €2,000–€10,000/mo for marketing alone. Accountants deliver P&Ls 3 months late. Ops consultants visit once and leave a binder. There's nothing integrated.
4. **Language barrier.** A restaurant in S'Agaró needs content in Catalan, Spanish, English, French, German. Currently impossible without expensive translation.
5. **Digital decay.** Website built 3 years ago, Google Business listing half-wrong, TripAdvisor unmanaged, reviews unanswered. Everything rots without ongoing attention.
6. **Blind flying.** Most owners don't know their real margins, their best-selling items' profitability, or where waste is happening. They feel busy but can't see clearly.
7. **Staff chaos.** Opening procedures live in someone's head. New hires take weeks to train. Checklists are paper or nonexistent. Nobody clocks in consistently.

### Ideal First Client Profile

- Boutique hotel or quality restaurant in Costa Brava
- Owner who cares about quality but has zero time for digital or admin
- Willing to pay for outcomes, not interested in learning tools
- DR is the living proof of concept — already running 60% of the full stack

## 3. Service Offering — Three Tiers

### Tier 1: Free Audit — "How Healthy Is Your Business?"

**Purpose:** Lead magnet. Show hospitality businesses not just their digital visibility gaps, but their operational and financial blind spots too.

**What's included:**
- **Visibility scan:** Google Search, Google Maps, ChatGPT, Perplexity, Gemini, Claude — screenshot evidence of how (or if) they appear for relevant queries
- **Competitor comparison:** How they stack up against 2–3 local competitors in visibility
- **Quick ops diagnostic:** Based on a 5-minute questionnaire — do they have digital SOPs? Staff checklists? Inventory tracking? Time tracking? Score out of 10.
- **Quick finance diagnostic:** Do they know their real margins? Do they reconcile POS weekly? Do they track supplier costs? Score out of 10.
- **Summary:** A single-page "business health score" across all three layers

**What's NOT included:**
- Recommendations (that's Tier 2)
- Any fixes or implementation

**Delivery:** Automated via agents. ~30 min human QA per report. Delivered as branded PDF + 15-min walkthrough call.

**Hook:** "You don't appear in ChatGPT" is good. "You're probably losing 15% margin to untracked waste" is better. Both together is the kill shot.

**Goal:** 100% conversion to "oh shit" moment across multiple dimensions. Client sees gaps they didn't know existed.

**[FRANC DECIDE]** Do we gate the audit behind an email/call, or make it fully self-serve on the website? Self-serve = more volume but lower quality leads. Gated = fewer but warmer.

### Tier 2: Business Health Audit (PAID — One-off or Monthly)

**Purpose:** Full diagnostic across all three layers. Tell them exactly what's broken and in what order to fix it.

**What's included:**

**Layer 1 — Visibility:**
- Full gap analysis across all digital touchpoints (website, GBP, directories, reviews, social, AI visibility)
- Competitive positioning: how they compare to top 5 local competitors
- Content strategy: what to publish, where, in which languages

**Layer 2 — Operations:**
- SOP gap analysis: which critical procedures are documented vs. in someone's head
- Staff workflow audit: clock-in/out, scheduling, task management — what exists, what's missing
- Menu engineering snapshot: which items are stars, puzzles, dogs (if POS data available)
- Inventory and waste assessment

**Layer 3 — Finance:**
- POS data health check: is data being captured correctly? What's missing?
- Margin analysis: estimated food/beverage costs vs. revenue (if data available)
- Cash flow visibility assessment: how quickly can the owner see yesterday's numbers?
- Supplier cost benchmarking (where data exists)

**Prioritized action plan:** What to fix first across all three layers. What matters most. What to ignore.

**What's NOT included:**
- Implementation (that's Tier 3)
- Website builds, content creation, or technical work
- Ongoing management

**Pricing:**
- One-off report: €500–€1,000 **[FRANC DECIDE]** exact price point
- Monthly retainer: €750–€2,000/mo depending on scope **[FRANC DECIDE]** — the expanded scope (ops + finance) justifies higher pricing than v1.0's €500–€1,500 range. Confirm.

**Delivery:** Agent-generated analysis with human strategic layer. 5–7 business days for initial report.

**Goal:** The client sees a clear picture of their entire business — not just marketing. They either fix it themselves (unlikely — they don't have time) or upgrade to Tier 3.
